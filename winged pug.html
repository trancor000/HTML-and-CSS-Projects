The 200 Most Unique Dog Names of 2020

Updated November 30, 2020 | Getting a Dog

ShareTweetPin It

Celebrities famously choose unique names for babies—Apple, Maddox, Blanket—while the rest of us tend to be a little more reserved. But when it comes to dog names, all bets are off.

Obscure Harry Potter references, world travel, sci-fi sidekicks—they all serve as inspiration for unique dog names. After all, our dogs are special, and they deserve personalized names just as much as personalized care.

Here at Rover, we reviewed our name database of over a million dogs and compiled this list of unique male and female dog names. Have fun!

50 Least Common Dog Names

These dog names are unusual because they fall at the bottom of the list of Rover’s top 1000 dog names. If you’re looking for a unique dog name, you won’t hear many of these at puppy kindergarten.

Note that we skipped spelling variants of common names, such as Bayleigh or Maxx, to focus on the true one-of-a-kind picks. Each of these names was given to at least 50 individual dogs.

Unique Dog Names: Baby Name Edition

Do you like the sound of a human baby name on a puppy? If so, you’re not alone. It used to be that most of us agreed on a good name for a dog. Fido, Rex, Lady: we expected generic choices. Now, however, you’re more likely to hear human names on dogs, from French bulldogs named Jacques to pit bulls named Bella and golden retrievers named Sheena.

But what are the most unusual people names for dogs? Here are the least common in Rover’s database—all given to at least 50 individual dogs. These are truly uncommon on dogs, as they fall well below even the top 1000 names.
